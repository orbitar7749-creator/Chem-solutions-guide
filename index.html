🧮 2. CONCENTRATION EXPRESSIONS

🎯 MEMORY: "MVWPMM"

M - V - W - P - M - M
Mass % | Volume % | W/V % | PPM | Mole Fraction | Molarity | Molality

"My Very Warm Puppy Makes Me Merry"
📊 Mass Percentage (w/w)
Mass % = (Mass of solute / Mass of solution) × 100
Unit: % (dimensionless)
Example: 10% glucose = 10g + 90g water
🧪 Volume Percentage (v/v)
Volume % = (V solute / V solution) × 100
Unit: % (dimensionless)
Example: 35% ethylene glycol (antifreeze)
⚖️ Mass by Volume % (w/v)
w/v % = (Mass solute (g) / Volume solution (mL)) × 100
Unit: % (g/mL)
Used in: Medicine & Pharmacy
🔬 Parts Per Million (ppm)
ppm = (Parts component / Total parts) × 10⁶
Unit: ppm
Example: 1 ppm fluoride in water
⚛️ Mole Fraction (x)
xᵢ = nᵢ / Σnᵢ    or    x₂ = n₂/(n₁+n₂)
Unit: None (0 to 1)
Key: Σx = 1 always!
🧴 Molarity (M)
M = n₂ / V(L)
Unit: mol/L or M
⚠️ TEMPERATURE DEPENDENT!
Volume changes with temperature
🌡️ Molality (m)
m = n₂ / Mass of solvent (kg)
Unit: mol/kg or m
✅ TEMPERATURE INDEPENDENT!
Mass never changes with T

🚨 Temperature Dependence Trick

✅ INDEPENDENT of T Mass %, ppm, Mole fraction, Molality
❌ DEPENDENT on T Molarity, Volume %

Memory: "MOLALITY has an 'L' for LOW temperature effect, MOLARITY has an 'R' for RISKY with temperature"

🌊 3. SOLUBILITY & HENRY'S LAW
Solubility Process Flow
Solute + Solvent
⬇️
Dynamic Equilibrium?
⬇️
✅ SATURATED
Rate in = Rate out
No more solute can dissolve
⚠️ UNSATURATED
Rate in > Rate out
More solute can dissolve
📐 Henry's Law
p = Kₕ × x
Where:
p = Partial pressure of gas
Kₕ = Henry's law constant (kbar)
x = Mole fraction of gas in solution

🔑 Key: Higher Kₕ = Lower solubility
🌡️ Temperature Effect
T ↑ ⇒ Kₕ ↑ ⇒ Solubility ↓
Why? Gas dissolution is exothermic
Result: Fish prefer cold water (more O₂)
📈 SOLID IN LIQUID
Temperature Effect:
• Depends on ΔH_sol
• Endothermic: T↑ solubility↑<
• Exothermic: T↑ solubility↓<

Pressure Effect:
• Negligible effect
(Solids & liquids incompressible)
📉 GAS IN LIQUID
Temperature Effect:
• Solubility ↓ with T↑<
(Dissolution is exothermic)

Pressure Effect:
• Solubility ↑ with P↑<
(Henry's Law)

Henry's Law Applications

  • 🥤 Soda bottles: Sealed under high pressure for more CO₂
  • 🤿 Scuba divers: Use He-O₂ mix (not N₂) to avoid "the bends"
  • 🏔️ High altitude: Low O₂ pressure → Anoxia
  • 🐟 Cold water: More dissolved O₂ → fish prefer cold
📉 4. RAOULT'S LAW & VAPOR PRESSURE
Raoult's Law Decision Tree
Is the solute VOLATILE?
⬇️
❌ NON-VOLATILE
Sugar, NaCl, Urea

Formula:
p₁ = x₁ × p₁°<
Only solvent contributes
✅ VOLATILE
Ethanol, Acetone

Formula:
p_total = x₁p₁° + x₂p₂°<
Both contribute!
VISUAL: Vapor Pressure Lowering
Pure Solvent
Surface = 100% solvent
+
Non-volatile Solute
Occupies surface space
⬇️
SOLUTION
Surface = Solvent + Solute
Vapor pressure DECREASES
📉 Relative Lowering of VP
(p₁° - p₁)/p₁° = x₂ = n₂/(n₁ + n₂)
For dilute solutions (n₂ ≪ n₁):
≈ (w₂ × M₁)/(M₂ × w₁)
💨 Vapor Composition
y₁ = p₁/p_total    y₂ = p₂/p_total
Key Rule: Vapor is always richer in the more volatile component!

💡 Raoult's vs Henry's Law

Both state: Partial pressure ∝ Mole fraction

Raoult's Law is a special case of Henry's Law where Kₕ = p₁°<
For ideal solutions, the proportionality constant is the pure component vapor pressure
⚖️ 5. IDEAL vs NON-IDEAL SOLUTIONS
✅ IDEAL SOLUTION
Obey Raoult's Law at ALL concentrations

• ΔH_mix = 0 (no heat change)
• ΔV_mix = 0 (no volume change)
• A-B = A-A = B-B interactions

Examples:
n-hexane + n-heptane
Benzene + Toluene
Bromoethane + Chloroethane
❌ NON-IDEAL SOLUTION
Deviate from Raoult's Law

• ΔH_mix ≠ 0
• ΔV_mix ≠ 0
• A-B ≠ A-A or B-B

Types:
Positive deviation (p > expected)
Negative deviation (p < expected)
Deviation Analysis
Compare A-B vs A-A/B-B forces
⬇️
📈 POSITIVE DEVIATION
A-B < A-A or B-B
(Weaker interactions)
ΔH_mix > 0 (Endothermic)
p > p_Raoult

Examples:
Ethanol + Acetone
CS₂ + Acetone
(H-bonds broken)
📉 NEGATIVE DEVIATION
A-B > A-A or B-B
(Stronger interactions)
ΔH_mix < 0 (Exothermic)
p < p_Raoult

Examples:
Phenol + Aniline
CHCl₃ + Acetone
(New H-bonds form)

Azeotropes (Constant Boiling Mixtures)

  • Minimum Boiling Azeotrope: Large positive deviation → boils at LOWER temp (e.g., Ethanol-Water, 95% ethanol)
  • Maximum Boiling Azeotrope: Large negative deviation → boils at HIGHER temp (e.g., HNO₃-Water, 68% HNO₃)
  • Key: Cannot separate by fractional distillation at azeotropic composition!
🧊 6. COLLIGATIVE PROPERTIES

🎯 MEMORY: "REDO"

R - E - D - O
Relative lowering of vapor pressure
Elevation of Boiling point
Depression of Freezing point
Osmotic pressure

"I need to REDO my colligative property notes!"
Depends on NUMBER of particles, NOT identity
Non-volatile solute required
Dilute solutions assumed
Used for Molar Mass determination
Property Formula Constant Used to Find Key Point
RLVP (p°-p)/p° = x₂ None M₂ (molar mass) Most fundamental
ΔT_b ΔT_b = K_b × m K_b (solvent) M₂ or m BP increases
ΔT_f ΔT_f = K_f × m K_f (solvent) M₂ or m FP decreases
Π Π = CRT R (gas const) M₂ (proteins!) Best for big molecules

⚡ Important Constants (WATER)

K_b (water)
0.52 K kg mol⁻¹
K_f (water)
1.86 K kg mol⁻¹
Normal BP
373.15 K (100°C)
Normal FP
273.15 K (0°C)
R (bar)
0.083 L bar K⁻¹ mol⁻¹
R (atm)
0.0821 L atm K⁻¹ mol⁻¹
🌡️ Elevation of Boiling Point
ΔT_b = K_b × m
m = (w₂ × 1000)/(M₂ × w₁)

M₂ = (K_b × w₂ × 1000)/(ΔT_b × w₁)
❄️ Depression of Freezing Point
ΔT_f = K_f × m
M₂ = (K_f × w₂ × 1000)/(ΔT_f × w₁)
💧 Osmotic Pressure
Π = CRT = (n₂/V)RT
M₂ = (w₂ × R × T)/(Π × V)

Best for: Proteins, polymers, biomolecules

💡 Why Osmotic Pressure is Best

  • Large magnitude even at low concentrations
  • Room temperature measurement
  • Uses molarity (easier than molality)
  • Biomolecules are heat-sensitive
⚡ 7. ABNORMAL MOLAR MASS & VAN'T HOFF FACTOR
Why Molar Mass is "Abnormal"?
Does solute DISSOCIATE or ASSOCIATE?
⬇️
🔨 DISSOCIATION
NaCl → Na⁺ + Cl⁻<
Particles INCREASE
i > 1
Observed M₂ < Actual

Examples:
KCl (i ≈ 2)
Na₂SO₄ (i ≈ 3)
🤝 ASSOCIATION
2CH₃COOH ⇌ (CH₃COOH)₂
Particles DECREASE
i < 1
Observed M₂ > Actual

Examples:
Acetic acid in benzene (i ≈ 0.5)
📐 Van't Hoff Factor (i)
i = Normal Molar Mass / Abnormal Molar Mass

i = Observed Colligative Property / Calculated Colligative Property

i = Total moles after / Total moles before
📉 Modified RLVP
(p₁° - p₁)/p₁° = i × x₂
🌡️ Modified ΔT_b
ΔT_b = i × K_b × m
❄️ Modified ΔT_f
ΔT_f = i × K_f × m
💧 Modified Π
Π = i × C × R × T
Salt Dissociation i (ideal) i (0.1 m) i (0.001 m)
NaCl Na⁺ + Cl⁻ 2 1.87 1.97
KCl K⁺ + Cl⁻ 2 1.85 1.98
MgSO₄ Mg²⁺ + SO₄²⁻ 2 1.21 1.82
K₂SO₄ 2K⁺ + SO₄²⁻ 3 2.32 2.84

Note: i approaches ideal value as solution becomes more dilute

🔄 8. OSMOSIS & REVERSE OSMOSIS
OSMOSIS DIRECTION
Pure Solvent
or Dilute
Lower concentration
→
Solution
or Concentrated
Higher concentration
THROUGH SEMIPERMEABLE MEMBRANE (SPM)
💧 OSMOSIS (Natural)
Solvent: LOW → HIGH concentration

• No external pressure needed
• Stops at equilibrium

Examples:
🥭 Mango shriveling in brine
🌸 Wilted flowers in water
🩸 Blood cells in saline
🔄 REVERSE OSMOSIS (Forced)
Solvent: HIGH → LOW concentration

• Needs P > Π (external pressure)
• Used for purification

Applications:
🌊 Sea water desalination
💧 Water purification
🏭 Industrial treatment

Tonicity Concepts

Isotonic Equal osmotic pressure → No net flow (0.9% NaCl = normal saline)
Hypertonic Higher pressure → Water flows OUT → Cell shrinks
Hypotonic Lower pressure → Water flows IN → Cell swells/bursts

Memory: "Hyper = High pressure, Hypo = Low pressure, Iso = Same"

🎯 10. EXAM STRATEGY & MEMORY COMPILATION

Ultimate Memory Compilation

  • "Like Dissolves Like": Polar→Polar, Non-polar→Non-polar
  • Henry's Law: Higher Kₕ = Lower solubility (resistance)
  • Raoult's Law: p ∝ x (vapor pressure proportional to mole fraction)
  • Colligative: "Collective" = depends on number, not identity
  • Van't Hoff: i>1 = dissociation, i<<1 = association
  • Boiling Elevation: Add solute → harder to boil → T↑
  • Freezing Depression: Add solute → harder to freeze → T↓
  • Osmotic Pressure: Best for BIG molecules (proteins)

🚨 Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing Molarity (M) with Molality (m)
  • Forgetting mL→L or g→kg conversions
  • Using total mass instead of solvent mass in molality
  • Not checking dissociation for salts (NaCl, KCl need 'i')
  • Forgetting mole fraction has no units
  • Using Celsius in gas laws (must be Kelvin!)
  • Confusing solution vapor pressure vs vapor phase composition
🎯 Key Formulas to Memorize
p = Kₕ·x  |  p₁ = x₁·p₁°  |  ΔT_b = iK_b·m  |  ΔT_f = iK_f·m  |  Π = iCRT
